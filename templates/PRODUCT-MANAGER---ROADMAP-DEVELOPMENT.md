### Ultimate Goal
**Primary Objective:** Develop a comprehensive, actionable product roadmap that aligns with business strategy, stakeholder needs, and market trends, achieving measurable improvements in product adoption, revenue growth, and user satisfaction within the next fiscal year.

## PHASE 1: INFORMATION GATHERING

### Required Inputs
1. **Input 1:** Target Product Vision - A concise statement of what the end product should achieve (e.g., "Launch a SaaS platform that streamlines project management for remote teams").
2. **Input 2:** Key Stakeholder List - Names, roles, and primary interests/requirements from executives to end-users.
3. **Input 3:** Market Research Findings - Insights on target market size, user needs, competitive landscape, and regulatory environment (2024-2025 data).
4. **Input 4:** Current Product Portfolio - Existing features, performance metrics, customer feedback, and revenue streams.

### Initial Assessment Checklist
- [ ] Verify all required inputs received.
- [ ] Validate input quality and completeness using stakeholder interviews and market research reports.
- [ ] Identify immediate red flags or blockers (e.g., regulatory constraints, resource limitations).
- [ ] Establish baseline metrics (current adoption rates, churn, feature usage).

## PHASE 2: RESEARCH & ANALYSIS

### Critical Knowledge Areas (15 Topics)

1. **Market Trends:** Latest consumer behavior shifts and industry disruptions in 2024.
2. **Competitive Landscape Analysis:** Strengths/weaknesses of top competitors using SWOT analysis.
3. **Product Strategy Frameworks:** Lean, Agile, or OKR methodologies for goal-setting.
4. **User Research Techniques:** Surveys, interviews, usability testing protocols.
5. **Roadmapping Tools & Platforms:** Free alternatives (e.g., Miro, Lucidchart) vs. premium options (e.g., Smartsheet).
6. **Agile Roadmap Execution:** Sprint planning and backlog refinement processes.
7. **Stakeholder Management:** Communication strategies for different audience segments.
8. **Risk Assessment Methods:** Qualitative/quantitative risk identification using AI tools.
9. **Product Analytics Metrics:** Key performance indicators (KPIs) to track roadmap impact.
10. **Customer Journey Mapping:** Visualizing user interactions across product stages.
11. **Value Proposition Development:** Crafting compelling value propositions for target segments.
12. **Roadmap Prioritization Techniques:** RICE, WSJF, or MoSCoW methods using AI prioritization algorithms.
13. **Regulatory Compliance Updates:** 2024-2025 changes affecting product design and launch (e.g., GDPR).
14. **AI Integration Opportunities:** Predictive analytics for demand forecasting, chatbots for user support, etc.

### Research Consolidation
After all sub-agents complete:
1. Synthesize findings into a unified strategy document.
2. Identify conflicts or contradictions in best practices across topics.
3. Prioritize recommendations by impact on business objectives and feasibility.
4. Create a master action plan with timelines and ownership assignments.

## PHASE 3: EXECUTION WORKFLOW

### Step-by-Step Process

**STEP 1: Vision Alignment Workshop**
- **Action:** Facilitate workshops to synthesize vision, stakeholder requirements, and market insights into a unified product vision statement.
- **Tools Needed:** Miro (free), Notion (optional), Google Docs
- **Success Criteria:** All stakeholders sign off on the final vision document within 2 weeks.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Misalignment between executive goals and user needs; scope creep during workshops.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 2: Market & Competitive Analysis**
- **Action:** Compile comprehensive market analysis report including TAM, SAM, SOM calculations, competitive feature matrix, and threat identification.
- **Tools Needed:** Gartner Magic Quadrant (optional), Crunchbase Pro (premium), Excel/Google Sheets
- **Success Criteria:** Completed analysis with validated assumptions; identified 3-5 key competitors analyzed in depth.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Inaccurate market sizing; overlooking emerging competitors.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 weeks

**STEP 3: Value Proposition Development**
- **Action:** Create value proposition statements for primary target segments, highlighting how the product solves their pain points and meets unmet needs.
- **Tools Needed:** Figma (free), Google Docs
- **Success Criteria:** 3-5 high-quality value propositions approved by product team lead.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Vague messaging; not addressing key user motivations.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 4: Roadmap Drafting**
- **Action:** Draft a high-level roadmap using selected tool (e.g., Miro, Lucidchart) outlining major milestones and dependencies.
- **Tools Needed:** Miro (free), Lucidchart (optional), Trello
- **Success Criteria:** Initial draft approved by stakeholders with clear labeling of phases and deliverables.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overambitious timelines; lack of granularity in feature planning.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 5: Stakeholder Review Cycle**
- **Action:** Conduct iterative review sessions with key stakeholder groups to refine roadmap, gather feedback, and make necessary adjustments.
- **Tools Needed:** Miro (free), Notion
- **Success Criteria:** All major stakeholders provide written feedback; changes incorporated into the latest version.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Inadequate communication leading to misaligned expectations.
- **Time Estimate:** Ongoing throughout roadmap development

**STEP 6: Prioritization Workshop**
- **Action:** Conduct a prioritization workshop using RICE or WSJF methodology to rank features and initiatives by impact and effort.
- **Tools Needed:** Excel (free), Airtable (optional)
- **Success Criteria:** Final priority list approved; all items categorized into high, medium, low priority.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overlooking dependencies between features; underestimating implementation complexity.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 7: Execution Planning**
- **Action:** Break down top-priority initiatives into sprints/schedule with clear deliverables, owners, and acceptance criteria.
- **Tools Needed:** Jira (free), Asana
- **Success Criteria:** Sprint plans approved; resource allocation plan aligned with team capacity.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overcommitment of resources; unclear ownership leading to bottlenecks.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 8: Communication & Roadshow Planning**
- **Action:** Develop communication strategy for internal stakeholders and external customers, including regular updates on roadmap progress.
- **Tools Needed:** Email templates (Google Docs), Social media planning tools (Buffer - free)
- **Success Criteria:** Quarterly roadshow agenda approved; content calendar published.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Lack of cadence in updates; not adapting messages to audience.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 9: Monitoring & Adjustments**
- **Action:** Implement KPI tracking for roadmap execution, using tools like Mixpanel or Amplitude for product metrics.
- **Tools Needed:** Mixpanel (free tier), Amplitude
- **Success Criteria:** Monthly review meetings with clear action items; any deviations from plan documented and addressed.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Not setting up dashboards early enough to capture relevant data.
- **Time Estimate:** Continuous

**STEP 10: Post-Launch Review**
- **Action:** Conduct a retrospective after the initial launch phase, comparing actual performance against planned outcomes.
- **Tools Needed:** Survey tools (Google Forms), Decision tracking templates
- **Success Criteria:** Identified gaps; documented lessons learned for future iterations.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Lack of post-launch analysis leading to unaddressed issues.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 month post-launch

## PHASE 4: OPTIMIZATION & REFINEMENT

### Performance Metrics
Define how to measure success:

1. **Primary Metric:** Product adoption rate (users who complete key actions) - Target: +15% YoY by Q3 2025.
2. **Secondary Metrics:**
   - Customer satisfaction score (CSAT): Target >85%
   - Revenue growth from new features: Target +20% QoQ
   - Time-to-market for critical updates: Target <8 weeks

### Iterative Improvement Loop
1. Measure current performance against targets after each sprint.
2. Identify top 3 improvement opportunities (e.g., user feedback, market shifts).
3. Implement changes in upcoming sprints.
4. Re-measure and document outcomes.

---

## PHASE 5: REPORTING & DOCUMENTATION

### Deliverables

**1. Executive Summary**
- Current state vs. target state
- Key actions taken
- Results achieved (e.g., adoption rate, revenue impact)
- ROI or impact metrics

**2. Detailed Roadmap Document**
- Complete methodology section explaining the research process.
- All research findings organized by topic area.
- Implementation details with timelines and owners.
- Before/after comparisons of key metrics.

**3. Maintenance Plan**
- Ongoing tasks to maintain roadmap alignment (e.g., quarterly stakeholder reviews).
- Monitoring schedule for KPIs.
- Update frequency (quarterly) and who is responsible.
- Contingency procedures for major pivots.

**4. Knowledge Transfer**
- Training materials on roadmap development process.
- Standard operating procedures for executing sprints.
- Best practices documentation stored in shared repository.
- Troubleshooting guide covering common issues like stakeholder misalignment or scope creep.
