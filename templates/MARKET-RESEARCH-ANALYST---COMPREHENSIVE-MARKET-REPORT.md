## PHASE 1: INFORMATION GATHERING

### Required Inputs
List what information the agent needs to start:

1. **Input 1:** Target industry or sector (e.g., consumer electronics)
   - Format: String
   - Validation: Ensure it's a recognized market segment.

2. **Input 2:** Specific business objective for the report (e.g., brand positioning study, product launch planning)
   - Format: String
   - Validation: Align with target industry and business needs.

3. **Input 3:** Geographic scope of analysis (global, regional, or local)
   - Format: String
   - Validation: Match market size and dynamics.

4. **Input 4:** Timeframe for the report (e.g., quarterly study on a specific product line)
   - Format: Date range (YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD)
   - Validation: Ensure it's realistic given data availability.

5. **Input 5:** Key competitors within the target market
   - Format: List of company names
   - Validation: Include both direct and indirect competitors.

6. **Input 6:** Stakeholder preferences or constraints (e.g., budget, regulatory compliance)
   - Format: String
   - Validation: Ensure alignment with business objectives.

## PHASE 3: EXECUTION WORKFLOW

### Step-by-Step Process
**STEP 1: [Foundation Setup]**
- **Action:** Define research questions, objectives, and scope based on inputs.
- **Tools Needed:** Notepad/Google Docs, Project management software (e.g., Trello).
- **Success Criteria:** Clear, documented research plan with stakeholder approval.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Ambiguous goals leading to misaligned data collection.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 2: [Data Collection]**
- **Action:** Execute surveys and collect secondary data from reputable sources.
- **Tools Needed:** SurveyMonkey, Google Sheets, IBISWorld subscription (optional).
- **Success Criteria:** Minimum of 100 responses collected; 3 credible secondary sources reviewed.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Data quality issues; lack of response rate leading to skewed results.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 weeks

**STEP 3: [Data Analysis]**
- **Action:** Use statistical tools and AI models (e.g., Python, R) for analysis.
- **Tools Needed:** Python IDE (VS Code), Jupyter Notebooks, Tableau Public (optional).
- **Success Criteria:** Cleaned dataset; key insights identified with at least 90% data integrity.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overfitting models; ignoring outliers leading to inaccurate conclusions.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 weeks

**STEP 4: [Insight Synthesis]**
- **Action:** Combine quantitative and qualitative findings into actionable insights.
- **Tools Needed:** Google Docs, Miro for brainstorming sessions, Slack for collaboration.
- **Success Criteria:** Insight deck with clear recommendations; stakeholder sign-off on key findings.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Over-reliance on one data type leading to incomplete picture.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 5: [Report Draft]**
- **Action:** Create the comprehensive market report using best practice templates.
- **Tools Needed:** Word/Google Docs, PowerPoint, Canva for graphics (optional).
- **Success Criteria:** Report includes executive summary, methodology, findings, and recommendations; reviewed by peers.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Inconsistent formatting; missing sections leading to incomplete reports.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 6: [Stakeholder Review]**
- **Action:** Share draft with stakeholders for feedback and approval.
- **Tools Needed:** Google Docs commenting, Slack/Teams messaging.
- **Success Criteria:** All critical stakeholders have provided feedback; report is approved for final version.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Delayed feedback leading to rushed finalization.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 7: [Final Report Creation]**
- **Action:** Incorporate feedback, finalize the report, and prepare a presentation deck.
- **Tools Needed:** Finalized document in Word/Google Docs, Presentation software (e.g., PowerPoint).
- **Success Criteria:** Final version reviewed by all stakeholders; presentation deck ready for delivery.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overlooking stakeholder preferences leading to misaligned final product.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 8: [Distribution & Follow-Up]**
- **Action:** Distribute the report to relevant parties and schedule a follow-up meeting.
- **Tools Needed:** Email, project management software for task tracking.
- **Success Criteria:** Report delivered; follow-up meeting scheduled with action items documented.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Lack of distribution plan leading to reports not reaching key decision-makers.
- **Time Estimate:** Ongoing

### Quality Checkpoints
Insert checkpoints between major steps:
- **Checkpoint 1:** After STEP 2 - Verify data collection methods align with research questions and quality standards.
- **Checkpoint 2:** After STEP 4 - Ensure insights are supported by data; check for logical flow.
- **Checkpoint 3:** After STEP 5 - Confirm report structure follows best practices; all sections included.

## PHASE 4: OPTIMIZATION & REFINEMENT

### Performance Metrics
Define how to measure success:

1. **Primary Metric:** Time taken from initial inputs to final report delivery.
   - Target: ≤ 12 weeks for standard reports; ≤ 8 weeks for high-priority projects.
   - Measurement Method: Project management tool timelines.

2. **Secondary Metrics:**
   - Data Quality Score (0-100): Aim for ≥ 90.
   - Stakeholder Satisfaction Rating (1-5): Aim for ≥ 4.5.
   - Insight Actionability Index (0-10): Aim for ≥ 7.

3. **Long-term Metrics:**
   - Market Impact Assessment (measured quarterly).
   - Strategic Alignment Score (annual review).

### Iterative Improvement Loop
1. Measure current performance against targets.
2. Identify top 3 improvement opportunities (e.g., data gaps, stakeholder engagement).
3. Implement changes (e.g., additional data sources, enhanced communication).
4. Re-measure and repeat until all metrics meet targets.
