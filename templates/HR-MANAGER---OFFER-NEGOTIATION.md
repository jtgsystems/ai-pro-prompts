### PHASE 1: INFORMATION GATHERING

#### Required Inputs
List what information the agent needs to start:

1. **Input 1:** Job Offer Details (salary range, benefits, equity options)
   - Format: Salary figures, bonus percentages, health insurance details, stock options, vacation policies
   - Validation: Ensure all figures are accurate and in USD

2. **Input 2:** Candidate Expectations (job responsibilities, growth opportunities, work-life balance preferences)
   - Format: List of desired roles/responsibilities, career progression expectations, preferred working hours
   - Validation: Confirm candidate has provided a clear list or we can infer from CV/resume

3. **Input 3:** Company Culture and Values
   - Format: Company mission statement, core values document, employee reviews on platforms like Glassdoor
   - Validation: Ensure information is current (last reviewed within the past year)

### PHASE 3: EXECUTION WORKFLOW

#### Step-by-Step Process

**STEP 1: [Initial Assessment]**
- **Action:** Compare offer details against candidate expectations and market benchmarks.
- **Tools Needed:** Salary comparison websites, budgeting software (e.g., Mint), spreadsheet for listing benefits.
- **Success Criteria:** Initial assessment score ≥80% indicates good alignment.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Ignoring non-monetary aspects like work-life balance.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 week

**STEP 2: [Identify Areas for Negotiation]**
- **Action:** List all negotiable items (salary, benefits, equity).
- **Tools Needed:** Spreadsheet with offer details vs. candidate expectations.
- **Success Criteria:** At least 3-5 areas identified for negotiation.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overlooking minor but significant aspects like health insurance coverage.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 days

**STEP 3: [Develop Negotiation Strategy]**
- **Action:** Create a draft negotiation plan including:
  - BATNA analysis
  - Preferred outcomes
  - Escalation path if needed
- **Tools Needed:** Drafting software (e.g., Google Docs), negotiation templates.
- **Success Criteria:** Strategy document approved by manager and candidate.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Lack of flexibility in approach.
- **Time Estimate:** 3 days

**STEP 4: [Prepare Candidate for Negotiation]**
- **Action:** Explain the strategy to the candidate, gather their input on key priorities.
- **Tools Needed:** Email templates, video conferencing software (e.g., Zoom).
- **Success Criteria:** Candidate feels informed and aligned with negotiation goals.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Miscommunication or lack of understanding from candidate.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 day

**STEP 5: [Conduct Negotiation Meeting]**
- **Action:** Hold a virtual meeting to discuss offer terms, present counter-offers, facilitate dialogue.
- **Tools Needed:** Video conferencing software, shared document for tracking points discussed.
- **Success Criteria:** All parties agree on revised offer details; candidate signs acceptance.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Emotional outbursts or lack of preparation from either side.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 day

**STEP 6: [Finalize Offer and Acceptance]**
- **Action:** Draft the final offer letter incorporating negotiated terms, send to candidate for acceptance.
- **Tools Needed:** Email templates, HR software (e.g., BambooHR).
- **Success Criteria:** Offer sent by deadline; response received within agreed timeframe.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Missing key signatures or overlooked details in final document.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 day

**STEP 7: [Incorporate Legal Review]**
- **Action:** Submit the finalized offer for legal review to ensure compliance with employment law and company policy.
- **Tools Needed:** Legal department portal, HR contract management software.
- **Success Criteria:** Offer approved by legal without amendments or flagged issues.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Hidden legal red flags uncovered after acceptance.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 days

**STEP 8: [Communicate Final Terms to Candidate]**
- **Action:** Send the final offer with all agreed terms, including start date and benefits schedule.
- **Tools Needed:** Email templates, HR software for scheduling onboarding documents.
- **Success Criteria:** Candidate confirms receipt of final offer and plans for start date.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Delayed communication leading to candidate confusion or loss interest.
- **Time Estimate:** 1 day

**STEP 9: [Onboarding Preparation]**
- **Action:** Coordinate with IT, finance, and other departments to prepare the onboarding process.
- **Tools Needed:** Project management software (e.g., Asana), HR welcome kit templates.
- **Success Criteria:** All necessary systems are active, background check processing begins.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Overlooking critical onboarding tasks like security clearances or IT setup.
- **Time Estimate:** 2 weeks

**STEP 10: [Final Sign-off and Celebration]**
- **Action:** Obtain written acceptance from the candidate, share congratulations with team.
- **Tools Needed:** Email templates, company social media for announcement.
- **Success Criteria:** Formal offer accepted; celebration event scheduled.
- **Common Pitfalls:** Lack of recognition leading to low morale among hiring team.
- **Time Estimate:** Immediate

### PHASE 4: OPTIMIZATION & REFINEMENT

#### Performance Metrics
Define how to measure success:

1. **Primary Metric:** Offer Acceptance Rate  
   - Target: ≥90% acceptance rate within the standard negotiation period (2 weeks)
   - Measurement Method: Track offers sent vs. accepted in HR system

2. **Secondary Metrics:**
   - Candidate Satisfaction Score: Conduct post-offer survey
     - Target: ≥85%
     - Measurement Method: SurveyMonkey or similar tool
   - Negotiation Timeframe Compliance:
     - Target: All negotiations completed within 4 weeks
     - Measurement Method: Timestamp logs

3. **Long-term Metrics:**
   - New Hire Performance Review Score after 6 months:
     - Target: ≥85% positive review rate
     - Measurement Method: Annual performance reviews

#### Iterative Improvement Loop
1. Measure current performance against targets.
2. Identify top 3 improvement opportunities (e.g., slow negotiation, high turnover).
3. Implement changes based on findings (e.g., better communication tools, updated offer templates).
4. Re-measure after implementation to confirm improvements.

4. **Knowledge Transfer**

   - Best practices documentation shared with the team
